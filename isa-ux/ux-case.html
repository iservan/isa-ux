Project Overview


Skills Assessment 2.0 [SA 2.0] serves as a key sales tool, allowing Red Hat clients to evaluate their team's technical proficiency in enterprise products. It identifies skill gaps, providing an opportunity to upsell customized training courses and certification packages, ensuring technical adoption.

The implementation of SA 2.0 involved close collaboration among four significant internal teams: Sales, Marketing, Curriculum and RHLS team (Red Hat Learning Subscriptions) which provides the technical capacities for developing and maintaining the learning platforms, of which I was a member. I collaborated extensively with all the stakeholders to gather business requirements and understand the user needs and pain points. I also worked closely with the engineer team to understand the high technical constraints and create a design that was both functional and visually appealing.

The SA 2.0 app was officially launched in June 2023.
Within the first month, Red Hat learning subscription sales increased over 15%. Since then, SA 2.0 has achieved more than 5,000 monthly visits.

My Role


I was entirely responsible for designing the User Experience and User Interface for the 2.0 version of the Skills Assessment. The entire architecture, codebase, user experience and visual design were built from the ground up.

I established the UX research strategy, employing diverse methods such as: stakeholder workshops, user surveys, in-dept thinking-aloud video sessions and usability testing with focus groups; to gather insights into business goals, user needs and technical requirements. These methods facilitated collaboration and alignment among teams, helped us empathize with users needs and ensured that the final product was intuitive and user-friendly.

Additionally, I consistently tracked and analyzed key metrics, (quantitative insights) using tools like Pendo, to promote data driven decision-making and understand user behavior, which ultimately boost user engagement, conversion and retentaion while driving business success.

Research and requirements optimisation


The Curriculum team had initiated the definition of business requirements, documented on an extensive spreadsheet.

My key task during this phase involved analyzing and validating the requirements to align them with user needs. I conducted workshops with key stakeholders from Marketing, Sales and Engineering to gather insights into business goals and technical requirements. These sessions facilitated collaboration and alignment among teams.

Challenge

Business requirements didn't considered user's needs

To understand user needs and friction points, I conducted qualitative UX research by creating UX surveys and organizing in-depth interviews, applying thinking aloud methodology, with current Red Hat clients. Through these sessions, I gathered insights on their experiences, frustrations and expectations during the Skill assessment process. By analyzing the qualitative data, I identified common pain points like the neeed to have a centralised endpoint to follow up with the team assesments answers.

The qualitative research not only informed the design decisions but also fostered empathy for the users, guaranteeing a user-centric approach.

Ideation - Prototyping


Utilizing insights gathered from previous phase, I created detailed user personas representing different segments of our target audience: technical managers (customers), technical members for these clients (final users) and Red Hat internal users from sales, marketing and curriculum teams. These three personas helped us empathize with users, understand their motivations, goals and pain points, guiding our design decisions. This led me to conceptualize three different user journeys tailored to meet the specific needs of each persona type.

Throughout this phase, I collaborated very closely with the technical team. These prototypes and wireframes served as valuable tools for clarifying requirements, facilitating collaboration, gathering feedback and optimising the development process.

Testing & validating


Prior to implementation, I conducted usability testing sessions with two focus users groups. By observing participants' interactions with prototypes and collecting feedback through structured tasks and open-ended questions, I identified a couple of usability concers like complex iteraction while filtering user's interests for the assessment . Iteratively I refined the design based on user feedback.

I was very intrigued to see that the pain points and features previously definend were solved in the prototype. It was interesting to see how the collaboration between the engineering and design tem lead to an app that not only meets technical standards but also delivers an optimised user experience.
