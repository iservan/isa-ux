Allstacks AI

Zero Maintenance Reporting

  • The Problem

Engineering teams were drowning in manual reporting tasks, spending hours aggregating data from multiple tools to answer stakeholder questions. Leaders lacked real-time visibility into project progress, risks, and resource allocation across their engineering organization.

  • The Outcome

We launched Allstacks AI, a conversational analytics platform that transforms how engineering teams communicate insights. The solution achieved zero maintenance reporting by automatically connecting data across tools, resulting in 85% reduction in manual reporting time and enabling teams to focus on building rather than reporting.

  • Role, Timeline & Team
  • 12 months
  • 3 cross-functional teams
  • 25 stakeholders
  • 8 engineers

I led the UX and UI design for Allstacks AI integration, collaborating across three key teams: Product, Engineering, and Data Science. My role encompassed the entire design process from initial concept to final implementation.

My responsibilities included conducting user research with engineering leaders, designing conversational interfaces that make complex data accessible, and creating intuitive dashboards that automatically surface actionable insights. I worked closely with our AI/ML team to ensure the natural language processing capabilities matched user mental models.

The AI platform was launched in Q3 2024, transforming how engineering teams communicate insights.
Within three months, we achieved zero maintenance reporting for 200+ engineering teams and reduced manual reporting time by 85%.

My approach focused on understanding the pain points of engineering leaders who spend countless hours manually creating reports.   • Discovery

Through interviews with 50+ engineering leaders, I discovered that teams spend 20+ hours weekly on manual reporting tasks. The key insight was that leaders needed conversational access to their data, not just another dashboard.

Research & Insights

I conducted comprehensive user research with engineering managers, directors, and VPs across various company sizes. The research revealed that while teams had access to data from multiple tools (Jira, GitHub, Slack, etc.), the process of aggregating and contextualizing this information was extremely time-consuming and error-prone.

Key Challenge

Data exists everywhere, insights exist nowhere
Engineering teams had abundant data but lacked the ability to quickly generate insights. Leaders were spending more time creating reports than analyzing the information to make strategic decisions. This led to delayed responses to critical issues and missed opportunities for optimization.
  • Key findings

Insight 1 Teams want to ask questions in natural language, not learn complex query languages or dashboard interfaces.

Insight 2 Context is crucial - the same metric means different things to different teams and at different times.

Insight 3 Leaders need both high-level summaries for executives and detailed data for operational decisions.

Insight 4 Real-time alerts and proactive insights are more valuable than historical reporting.

Insight 5 Integration across tools is essential - insights should connect data from all engineering systems.

  • Ideation

I defined three primary personas: Engineering Managers (day-to-day operations), Engineering Directors (strategic planning), and C-level executives (high-level insights). Each needed different interaction patterns and information depth.

User Personas

Based on research insights, I created detailed personas representing the key users of our AI platform:

  • Engineering Managers Frontline leaders managing day-to-day operations
  • Goal Quickly identify and resolve blockers, optimize team performance
  • Needs Real-time visibility into sprint progress, team workload, and risk identification
  • Value Faster decision-making and proactive issue resolution
  • Engineering Directors Strategic leaders planning resources and roadmaps
  • Goal Optimize resource allocation and predict delivery timelines
  • Needs Cross-team insights, capacity planning, and trend analysis
  • Value Data-driven strategic planning and improved predictability
  • C-Level Executives Senior leaders focused on business outcomes
  • Goal Understand engineering impact on business objectives
  • Needs High-level summaries, ROI insights, and executive-ready reports
  • Value Clear connection between engineering activities and business results

These personas guided the design of different interaction modes within the AI platform, ensuring each user type could access information in the most relevant format for their decision-making needs.

  • Prototyping

I created conversational flow prototypes that demonstrated how users could interact with their data through natural language. The key was designing an interface that felt like talking to a knowledgeable team member rather than querying a database.

Conversational Flows

The core innovation was designing conversational interfaces that could understand context and provide relevant insights. I mapped out various user journeys, from simple queries to complex multi-step analysis.

I worked closely with the AI/ML team to understand the capabilities and limitations of our natural language processing, ensuring the user experience matched the technical reality while pushing the boundaries of what was possible.

Design Innovation

Context-aware conversational analytics

The breakthrough was designing an AI that doesn't just answer questions but understands the context behind them. For example, when someone asks about "delivery risk," the system considers current sprint status, historical patterns, team capacity, and external dependencies to provide actionable insights.

Interface Wireframes

I designed the interface to seamlessly blend conversational AI with visual data representation. The challenge was creating an experience that felt natural for both asking questions and consuming complex analytical insights.

The wireframes focused on progressive disclosure, where users could start with simple questions and drill down into more detailed analysis as needed. This approach supported both quick checks and deep analytical sessions.

  • UI Design

AI-First Design Language

I developed a design system specifically for AI-powered analytics, emphasizing clarity, trust, and intelligence. The visual language needed to convey both the sophistication of the AI and the reliability of the data.

Key design principles included progressive disclosure, contextual help, and clear indication of AI confidence levels. The interface uses subtle animations and visual cues to guide users through complex analytical workflows.

  • Testing & Validation

We conducted extensive testing with engineering teams to validate both the AI accuracy and the user experience. The testing revealed that users quickly adapted to conversational analytics, with 90% of participants preferring it over traditional dashboards.

User Testing Results

Beta testing with 15 engineering teams showed remarkable results. Teams reported that the AI understood their questions correctly 95% of the time and provided actionable insights that directly influenced their decision-making.

The most significant validation came from observing how teams integrated Allstacks AI into their daily workflows. Rather than checking dashboards periodically, teams began having ongoing conversations with their data, leading to more proactive and informed decisions.

  • Impact & Results

Since launch, Allstacks AI has transformed how 200+ engineering teams access and act on their data. We achieved zero maintenance reporting and reduced manual reporting time by 85%, allowing teams to focus on building rather than reporting.
