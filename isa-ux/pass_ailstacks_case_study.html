My design process focused on understanding how engineering leaders currently consume and act on data, then designing AI interfaces that feel natural and trustworthy. The process emphasized rapid prototyping of conversational interfaces, continuous validation with real users, and ensuring AI transparency to build user confidence in automated insights.

  • Discovery

Through user interviews and workflow analysis, I identified key pain points in how engineering leaders interact with business intelligence data. The insights revealed opportunities for AI to transform complex data interpretation into intuitive, conversational experiences.

Research & Insights

I conducted in-depth interviews with 15 engineering managers and technical leaders to understand their current reporting workflows and decision-making processes. These sessions revealed significant friction points in translating technical metrics into business value, with leaders spending hours manually analyzing dashboards and creating reports.

Project Challenge

Complex data interpretation was blocking strategic decisions
Engineering leaders were spending hours manually analyzing dashboards and creating reports, often missing critical insights buried in complex visualizations. The gap between raw data and actionable insights was preventing teams from connecting their work to business outcomes and making strategic decisions.
  • Key findings
Insight 1
Leaders wanted natural language interfaces to query complex datasets without learning dashboard navigation
Insight 2
Teams needed AI-generated summaries that automatically identify trends and anomalies in performance metrics
Insight 3
Custom report configuration was too complex, requiring a more intuitive, speech-based approach
Insight 4
Decision-makers needed confidence indicators for AI-generated insights to trust recommendations
Insight 5
Users wanted AI explanations that show reasoning behind insights to build trust and enable informed decisions

  • Ideation

I defined three core user personas and designed AI interaction patterns that match their natural workflow. The focus was on creating conversational interfaces that feel intuitive while handling complex business intelligence queries.

User persona

Utilizing insights gathered from previous phase, I created detailed user personas representing different segments of our target audience who would interact with AI-powered intelligence features:

These three personas helped us empathize with users, understand their motivations, goals and pain points when interacting with AI systems, guiding the design decisions for conversational interfaces. This led me to conceptualize three different AI interaction journeys tailored to meet the specific needs of each persona type.

  • Engineering Managers
  • Technical leaders from engineering teams who need to connect team performance to business outcomes
  • Goal Connect team performance to business outcomes through data-driven insights
  • Needs Quick insights from complex datasets without manual analysis and AI-generated summaries
  • Value AI-generated summaries that highlight actionable trends.
  • Technical Leaders
  • Senior engineers and architects who make strategic technical decisions
  • Goal Make data-driven strategic decisions with confidence
  • Needs Natural language querying of technical metrics and speech-based report configuration
  • Value Conversational AI that eliminates complex dashboard navigation.
  • Executive Stakeholders
  • C-level and VP-level leaders who need to understand technical team impact on business goals
  • Goal Understand technical team impact on business goals without technical complexity
  • Needs Clear, actionable intelligence presented in business language with confidence indicators
  • Value AI-interpreted dashboards that translate technical metrics to business language.

Insight

Speech-based configuration became the breakthrough feature.

While building conversational AI interfaces, we initially focused on text-based natural language queries. During prototyping it became clear that speech-based report configuration had transformational potential, allowing users to describe complex analytical needs as naturally as talking to a colleague.
