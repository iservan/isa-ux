Project Overview


REalyse is a technology data platform that helps real estate developers, lenders, consultants, and investors understand where, when, and what to build or buy.

I'm playing a vital role in refining our newest product, the Portfolio Monitoring tool. My job is to make sure our features meet user needs and drive engagement. I've applied a range of industry best practices UX research methods to deliver impactful results.

REalyse's Portfolio Monitoring Tool (PMT) is in its early days, but is witnessing daily consistent and engaged traffic from users who find value in its portfolio monitoring functionalities.

UX Research


Utilizing quantitative analytics tools, I've gained invaluable Data-Driven Insights to inform our design decisions. By analyzing user behavior metrics, such as engagement rates and feature usage patterns, I've been able to pinpoint areas for improvement and prioritize enhancements that truly resonate with our audience.

Additionally, I've conducted comprehensive UX surveys and in-depth interviews to dig deeper into user needs and preferences. These qualitative research methods have provided rich insights into previously unaddressed user pain points, allowing us to guarantee an center-design solution.

Ideation & Prototyping


The problem:

After conducting a qualitative UX survey, I found out that the users, really needed to see property images while browsing through the "Refine comparables" view, just to identify the property and have a quick reference. So, I needed to make sure this feature didn't disrupt their flow while selecting comparables.

After brainstorming some ideas, I created interactive prototypes to test with a select group of survey participants. We ran an A/B test and one option clearly stood out as the winner. I took the feedback gathered during testing and fine-tuned the design. Following this process, we were confident that the feature was user-friendly and nailed exactly what our users were looking for.

Solution

Display property main image on one click + Load full images gallery on second clik interaction

Challenge

Relying exclusively on email communication to test prototypes.

Due to time constraints during the testing phase, we relied solely on email communication to share prototypes. Users struggled with the Figma interface, causing confusion and delays due to numerous email threads. Realizing the limitations of this approach, I switched to conducting video sessions with users, employing the thinking aloud methodology. While video sessions seem a more time-consuming approach, they enable to gain deeper insights into user interactions, frustration and friction points.

Since adopting this approach, the testing process has become more productive, genereting more valuable feedback while fostering empathy for the users, resulting in a more user-centric approach.
